Research

Realizability

My main research field is classical realizability. I study its application to classical set theory.

My thesis' goal is to find an explicit realizer for the Axiom of Choice, analysing Krivine's realizability models for ZF. At the same time, I try to realize other relevant principles which arise from set theory, like the Partition Principle or the Axiom of Constructivity.

λ-calculus

As I deal with the computational content of (classical) mathematics, I am also interested in λ-calculus and its extentions.

Historical stuff

I am also engaged into tracing back the development of realizability. This technique was conceived in 1945 by S.C.Kleene, and it has been applied in several - and more important, different - forms since then.
